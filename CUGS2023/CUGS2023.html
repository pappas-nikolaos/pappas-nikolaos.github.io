PhD Research Project

Semantics of Information in Neuromorphic Computing

 

Project leader and supervisor

Nikolaos Pappas

 

Context and Research Area

Neuromorphic computing is an emerging computing paradigm that goes towards the online, event-driven, processing of streaming data, departing from the classical batched processing. Furthermore, neuromorphic sensing and computing are brain-inspired paradigms for efficient data collection and semantic processing. The main features of the technology include energy efficiency, native event-driven processing of time-varying sources, spike-based computing. Neuromorphic chips, when coupled with spike-based sensors, can inherently adapt to the semantics of information by consuming less energy only when relevant events are recorded in the timing of spikes and by providing low-latency responses to the dynamics of the environment. Neuromorphic processors, also known as spiking neural networks (SNNs), are networks of dynamic spiking neurons that mimic the operation of biological neurons.

 

Research Methodology and Expected Results

We will explore the semantics of information in distributed neuromorphic computing systems. Timing, captured by Age of Information (AoI) and its variants, will serve as a proxy for semantics. The impact of timing metrics on distributed neuromorphic computing remains largely unexplored. For instance, in neuromorphic cameras, we will report significant changes at the image or mesoscopic level instead of pixel-level changes. This can lead to information reduction, even down to zero transmission, as periods of silence can convey the absence of significant change. When certain thresholds are exceeded, the end device can request new information—a pull-based method not yet considered in research. We'll use Markov Chains to encode the system's states, investigating their dimensions and granularity. Markov Chains capture temporal correlation and efficiently encode source behavior. The challenge lies in reconstructing the source's current state on a remote device (e.g., robotic arm) for control actions. Semantics will guide efficient modeling of Markov Chains, taking different modes into account (e.g., alarm or normal) that require varying levels of information refinement and timeliness. We'll define metrics for Semantics of Information, extending the current AoI, non-linear AoI, and Age of Actuation. Prediction schemes and optimization methods like MDP and Partial Observable MDP will be employed to track system evolution and reduce information handling. Reinforcement Learning (RL) and Deep RL techniques, such as actor-critic methods and hierarchical RL, will be utilized.

 

 

Qualifications of Prospective PhD Student

The main qualifications are as indicated in the CUGS announcement for the PhD student positions.

For this project, it is meriting if the applicants have some background in stochastic modeling and optimization such as Markov Decision Processes. It is also meriting if the applicants have a background in Reinforcement Learning (RL) and/or Deep RL.

The project will rely on the candidate having the ability to develop and debug software independently.

When selecting the candidate, we will also consider verbal and written English language proficiency.
